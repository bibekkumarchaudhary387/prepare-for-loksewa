Choose the correct alternative to improve the sentence:

If Nepal had the necessary expertise on water resources, the country should be producing electricity in a huge amount.

Correct Answer: the country would produce electricity in a huge amount

Explanation:
This sentence talks about a hypothetical situation — something that isn’t true right now but could be imagined.

For these “if” sentences (called second conditional), the pattern is:
If + past tense, ... would + base verb.

So, instead of saying “should be producing,” which sounds awkward here, we say “would produce” to show the imagined result.

Put simply, it means: “If Nepal had the skills, then it would produce a lot of electricity.”

Why the other options don’t work:

  • the country will produce electricity in a huge amount – “Will” is for real future events, not hypothetical ones.
  • the country is producing electricity in a huge amount – Present tense doesn’t fit the hypothetical “if” clause.
  • No change is necessary – The original sentence’s verb phrase doesn’t match the conditional grammar.

If x + 5 = 7, then 3x + 1 = ?

Correct Answer: 7

Explanation:
First, solve for x:
x + 5 = 7 → x = 7 - 5 → x = 2.

Now substitute x = 2 into the expression 3x + 1:
3(2) + 1 = 6 + 1 = 7.

So, the answer is 7.

This is a basic example of solving linear equations by isolating the variable and then substituting back.

If 12 persons can complete a work in 4 days, in how many days can 4 persons complete the work?

Correct Answer: 12

Explanation:
The total amount of work stays the same, no matter how many people are working.

First, calculate the total work in person-days:
12 persons × 4 days = 48 person-days.

Now, if 4 persons are working, divide total work by number of persons to find the days needed:
48 person-days ÷ 4 persons = 12 days.

So, 4 persons will take 12 days to finish the work.

This is a classic example of work and time problems where the product of workers and days is constant.

The sum of a, a + 4 and a + 5 is 12, then the value of a is .....

Correct Answer: 1

Explanation:
We are given the sum:
a + (a + 4) + (a + 5) = 12.

Combine like terms:
a + a + 4 + a + 5 = 12 → 3a + 9 = 12.

Subtract 9 from both sides:
3a = 12 - 9 → 3a = 3.

Divide both sides by 3:
a = 3 ÷ 3 → a = 1.

So, the value of a is 1.

This is a straightforward example of solving linear equations by simplifying and isolating the variable.
