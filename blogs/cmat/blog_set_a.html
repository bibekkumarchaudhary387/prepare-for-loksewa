If x + 5 = 7, then 3x + 1 = ?

Correct Answer: 7

Explanation:
First, solve for x:
x + 5 = 7 → x = 7 - 5 → x = 2.

Now substitute x = 2 into the expression 3x + 1:
3(2) + 1 = 6 + 1 = 7.

So, the answer is 7.

This is a basic example of solving linear equations by isolating the variable and then substituting back.

If 12 persons can complete a work in 4 days, in how many days can 4 persons complete the work?

Correct Answer: 12

Explanation:
The total amount of work stays the same, no matter how many people are working.

First, calculate the total work in person-days:
12 persons × 4 days = 48 person-days.

Now, if 4 persons are working, divide total work by number of persons to find the days needed:
48 person-days ÷ 4 persons = 12 days.

So, 4 persons will take 12 days to finish the work.

This is a classic example of work and time problems where the product of workers and days is constant.

Correct Answer: 1

Explanation:

a + (a + 4) + (a + 5) = 12.

Combine like terms:
a + a + 4 + a + 5 = 12 → 3a + 9 = 12.

Subtract 9 from both sides:
3a = 12 - 9 → 3a = 3.

Divide both sides by 3:
a = 3 ÷ 3 → a = 1.

So, the value of a is 1.

This is a straightforward example of solving linear equations by simplifying and isolating the variable.

Stainless Steels which are resistant to corrosion and oxidation typically needs

Stainless steels are resistant to corrosion and oxidation primarily due to the presence of chromium. Typically, a stainless steel must contain at least 10.5% chromium. What is Nepal's biggest import item?

Nepal's largest import item is mineral fuels, including oil, which accounted for approximately 23% of total imports in 2023. This category primarily comprises refined petroleum products, including diesel, kerosene, and gasoline, which are essential for transportation, electricity generation, and household energy needs. The majority of these petroleum products are imported from neighboring India, facilitated through the Nepal Oil Corporation.

The high demand for petroleum products in Nepal is driven by factors such as the transportation sector, which consumes over 62% of the petroleum products, and the increasing energy requirements of the country. This dependency on imported oil contributes significantly to Nepal's trade deficit.
